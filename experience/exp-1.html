Overview

The Small Satellite Laboratory's CubeSat Digital-Twin Research Program at Cal Poly Pomona aimed to develop a software architecture, hardware testbed, and simulative testing environment to support digital-twin technology for the school's CubeSat project. This consisted of the creation of a custom 3-DOF (Degrees Of Freedom) mechanically controllable gyroscope to mount the CubeSat, a control architecture for the gyroscope, digital-twin simulation software, and digital testing domain.

I was responsible for the research and development of the mechnical gyroscope. After months of conceptual design and prototyping, I, among a couple other student researchers, pitched the idea of the 3-DOF CubeSat gyro platform to Quanser Inc. and were able to secure approval and development of it, as well as Quanser as a funding partner for the research.

While the custom gyro was being developed, I built a working control architecture for it using MATLAB and Simulink. It can control the position, rotational speed, acceleration, and direction of the gyro. I used a commercially bought Quanser gyro to test this control scheme. I performed many open- and closed-loop tests, making hardware response times and transient states as fast as possible, as well as steady-state values as accurate as possible to input values. Furthermore, I identified and troubleshot many issues with the Quanser hardware itself, having to recalibrate faulty internal sensors and actuators.

After the control architecture, I co-authored preliminary digital-twin testing procedures that outlined different testing schemes to perform on the CubeSat and broadly how to do it. The procedures were based on best practices outlined within the NASA Operational Simulator for Space Systems (NOS3) guidelines for integration between physical and cirtual models, recommended tests to perform, and how to gather data properly. The idea was to provide a framework for students carrying on the program with the intention of further developing it, once details of the project get more fleshed-out.
