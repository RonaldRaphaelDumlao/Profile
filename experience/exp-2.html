Overview

The NASA L'SPACE Mission Concept Academy is a NASA workforce development program that teaches its members how to develop a space mission and mechanical space vehicle, based on current NASA standards and practices. The program does this by forming large teams, and conducting individual screenings to determine each member's role in their respective team. Goals and objectives are presented, along with various requirements and constraints, that outline the basis and foundation of the mission, and it is up to the teams to determine the best way to accomplish the mission. The program also provides access to many industry standard software, such as Siemens NX CAD, JMARS, NASA PCEC & MOCET, and various handbooks.

I had the honor and responsibility of being a Project Manager for my team of 16. I took on many managerial tasks, such as work flow organization, task distribution, documentation, organizing and leading meetings, scheduling, progress checks, and deliverable reviews.

Aside from that, I was also heavily involved in the development of the technical work, such as developing the work-breakdown structure to establish a digestible visual diagram of the project's objectives, milestones, and deliverables. I was also responsible for the development of a total of 91 system-level and derived requirements for the mission and our space vehicle that drove project objectives and scope, created a verification matric for our vehicles 5 different subsystems, as well as ensuring that each requirement was satisfied under a verification plan. Additionally, I developed a system interface control matrix to monitor how each of our vehicle's components interfaced with each other physically and through software, as well as used Siemens NX to model some of our vehicle's components, full assembly, and final render.

My team's mission was to send a rover on a discover-class mission to Mars to research and analyze its icy regions and atmosphere, in order to establish further argument that life can be sustained there. We were able to successfully pass through 4 different design reviews: Mission Concept Review (MCR), System Requirements Review (SRR), Mission Definition Review (MDR), leading up to a Preliminary Design Review (PDR). We also performed a 30 minute presentation in front of a panel of 7, consisting of engineers, scientists, and managers from NASA JPL. At the end of the program, out of 36 different teams, we scored the 4th highest rated PDR.
