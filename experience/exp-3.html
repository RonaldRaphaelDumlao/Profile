Overview

The NASA L'SPACE Proposal Writing Academy is a NASA workforce development program that teaches the fundamentals of scientific research and conceptualization of a novel technology. The program forms teams of its candidates, conducting individual screenings to determine each member's role in their respective team. Several Requests for Proposal (RFP) are presented, which represent real technological gaps that NASA is currently struggling to fill. It is then up to the teams to come up with an original invention that addresses their chosen RFP, which will be developed into a final technical proposal that could earn the team a $10K NASA grant to physically develop their proposed technology.

At the start of the program, each member of a team individually sumbits a quad chart, which describes and pitches an original idea for new technology fulfilling an RFP, and they get sent to a panel of engineers and scientists at NASA Marshall Space Flight Center. After some deliberation, the panel sends their chosen quad chart back to each team. I had the privilege of getting my concept of a novel solar thermal propulsion technology for small satellites chosen, which made me the Principal Investigator of my team.

As essentially the lead researcher on my team, my responsibilities were heavily technical, as well as managerial. My job was to have my team understand my technological concept, and to further develop it into a realistic model, backed by scientific research. Along with research, I developed 16 preliminary system-level requirements that drove scope of the development process, identified 21 potential risks of the system, and developed mitigation plans for each risk case. I also conducted market analysis and trade studies against 5 current state-of-the-art systems, with numbers showing that my concept was potentially 9% cheaper than the current cheapest option, just as propellant efficient as the second most efficient option, but can sustain spacecraft for longer missions up to 147% longer than the current best option. This analysis allowed me to easily justify technological demand for my concept.Additionally, I used SolidWorks to model a 3D prototype of the thruster, showing the body and nozzle, propellant feed lines, and thrust production mechanisms.

My team was able to submit a thorough technical proposal outlining the technological need of our system, how our system compares to existing technology and why it's better, potential system risks and mitigations, estimated performance metrics of our system, technology development work plan and project schedule if we were to get funding, and the necessary hardware, software, and facilities we'd need to build a prototype. Although we did not recieve funding for our concept, out of 21 teams, we scored the 5th highest rated proposal.
